    • Woah Yeah!

    My name's Justin Jaewon Kim~ I'm a UX designer (that's where jujakide comes from!) and a play/screen writer on the side.

    Recently graduated from UCSD for cognitive science and ready to start working full-time opportunities, especially in game development. I love to create memorably playful experiences using human-centered design principles. My itch for storytelling has resulted in some plays and screenplays you can read.

    Good writers (and designers) are interested in people, so I'd love to hear from you! Email, Twitter, and LinkedIn all work for me!

    1. UCSD Global Ties: Diabeatit

    Diabeatit is an educational health game aiming to teach children about diabetes. Made under UCSD Global Ties's Design For Development Lab for clients at the UCSD School of Medicine.

    UX and UI Design, Game Development

    2. Power Trip UCSD

    Power Trip UCSD is a satirical interactive fiction (IF) game about abusing your power as UCSD's latest chancellor. Made as a side project in Twine with memes and procrastination.

    UI Coding, UX Design, Game Development

    3. FeetFit Kiosk

    FeetFit is a point of purchase (POP) shoe kiosk made for UCSD's Prototyping studio that aims to accurately measure the users' feet and quickly deliver the right shoes.

    UX and Graphic Design

    4. Design For America: The Buddy Book

    The Buddy Book is a handbook for helping K-12 refugee students in San Diego integrate into American life by giving a list of fun and helpful activities they can do together.

    UX and Graphic Design

    5. Come Together

    Come Together is an app for community empowerment via crowd-sourcing that addresses difficulties with fixing problems in local neighborhoods. Made for an Interaction Design class under the Crowd-Sourcing studio.

    UX and UI Design
